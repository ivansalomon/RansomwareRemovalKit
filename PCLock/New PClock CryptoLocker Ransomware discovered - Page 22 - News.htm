Since most of the questions are duplicates I decided to create a short compilation of frequently asked questions. If you are going to ask any of these questions, be prepared to just be pointed towards this post again. I usually prefer to answer everyone personally but due to the large amount of requests I unfortunately can't keep up otherwise.

The decrypter complains that it could not find the decryption key required to decrypt my files. What is going on?
Most of the malware variants use a unique key to encrypt your files. In order to decrypt your files properly the decrypter needs to recover that key first. The error message you received indicates that the decrypter wasn't able to find the required key for your system. The most common circumstances under which this error occurs are:
  • Your files were encrypted by a variant that isn't supported yet or where we haven't recovered all secret keys yet. For further information please take a look at the following two questions.
  • You or one of your security programs removed the malware's configuration data from the system.
  • You re-installed Windows without backing up the enc_files.txt and malware configuration data first.
In the first case it is worth keeping an eye out for new and updated versions of the decrypter. In the other two cases there is unfortunately nothing I can do.

How many malware variants are there and what is their decryption status?
At the moment there are various different variants of the malware in circulation. I am happy to say that we can help in the majority of cases. This is the current breakdown:
  • Files encrypted by variant 1 can be restored in all cases.
  • Files encrypted by variant 2 can be restored in about 95% of all cases.
  • Files encrypted by variant 3 can be restored in about 95% of all cases.
  • Files encrypted by variant 4 can be restored in about 1% of all cases.
  • Files encrypted by variant 5 can be restored in about 95% of all cases.
  • Files encrypted by variant 6 can be restored in about 95% of all cases.
  • Files encrypted by variant 7 can be restored in about 95% of all cases.
I am actively looking into variant 4 at the moment, but it is unlikely that I will be able to provide decryption for it at all.

Will paying the ransom give me back access to my files?
It will not. The malware author will gladly take your money, but he will not be able to decrypt your files. Rule of thumb in case of this specific malware family is: If the decrypter is unable to decrypt your files, then the malware is unable to decrypt your files as well. So do not pay under any circumstances.

How can I figure out which malware variant targeted my files?
To figure out which variant encrypted your files, please follow these steps:
  • Press your R key while holding down your Windows key to bring up the "Run" window.
  • Copy the following command to the edit box and click OK:
    "%AppData%\WinCL\winclwp.jpg"
  • The malware's wallpaper graphic should open now. If it does not it either means you were never infected with PClock, you or one of your security applications removed that file, or you re-formatted your system.
  • The interesting part for us is the beginning of the download link provided in the wallpaper picture:



    If the URL in the picture starts with "http://invisioncorp." your files were encrypted by variant 1 or 2. If the URL in the picture starts with "http://korworks." your files were encrypted by variant 3 (contains "/wl/" later in the URL), 5 (contains "/wl2/" later in the URL), 6 (contains "/wl3/" later in the URL) or 7 (contains "/wl4/" later in the URL). If the URL in the picture starts with "http://vgresgrweu5vpucb." your files were encrypted with variant 4.
If your picture starts with an URL that isn't listed above, please contact me immediately via PM as you may have been affected by a completely new variant in that case.

Can I re-install Windows and keep the encrypted files in case a solution is found later?
The encryption of your files partially depends on a few infection specific information that you must backup before you can re-install Windows safely. If you do not backup these vital information you will not be able to decrypt your files even if a solution is found later. To backup the required information follow these steps:
  • Press the R key while holding down the Windows key to open the "Run" window.
  • Type in the following command:
    reg export "HKCU\Software\VB and VBA Program Settings\CLOCK" "%USERPROFILE%\backup_me.reg"
  • Click "OK". A black window will show up for a brief moment and immediately close. That is completely normal. So don't be too confused by that.
  • Press the Windows key and R to the "Run" window again.
  • Type in the following command this time:
    "%USERPROFILE%"
  • Click "OK" again. This time the Windows Explorer window should open with your user profile folder being the current folder. You will find 2 files there: "enc_files.txt" and "backup_me.reg". Copy both those files to a safe place like a USB stick.
After you have saved both files to a safe place you can safely perform a Windows re-install.

I decrypted my files. What should I do next?
The next step is to clean and secure your system from any other malware infections. BleepingComputer has an excellent team of volunteers that will gladly help you clean your system. Further information about how to initiate the malware removal process can be found here.

Some of my files have a size of 0 bytes or are partially corrupt after decryption!
The malware unfortunately contains several bugs that can cause files to be truncated or damaged. There is unfortunately nothing I can do in those cases at the moment.

I would like to support your cause! Do BleepingComputer or you accept donations?
BleepingComputer as a community doesn't accept donations at the moment and neither do I. I suggest you put the money you would have donated towards a proper backup solution instead so you are prepared next time something like this happens .
